## The one-line mission

Sports Today helps people understand **what matters today** — a calm daily companion, not another dashboard. The rest of this document is why that matters and how we hold the line.

---

# The Mission

Sports Today helps people understand **what matters today.**

It is not designed to replace ESPN.

It is not designed to replace StatMuse.

It is not designed to replace sportsbooks.

Its purpose is to answer one simple question:

> **If I have five minutes today, what should I pay attention to?**

Every screen should make answering that question easier.

---

# The Problem

Modern sports fans have access to unlimited information.

Scores.

Standings.

Odds.

Injuries.

Stats.

Highlights.

Podcasts.

Predictions.

The problem is not access.

The problem is knowing what deserves attention.

Sports Today exists to reduce that cognitive load.

Instead of showing everything...

...it curates what matters.

---

# Product Philosophy

Sports Today is a companion.

Not a destination.

Users should open the app for a few minutes each day.

Quickly understand today's sports landscape.

Leave feeling informed.

The application should respect the user's time.

---

# The Core Experience

Every visit should answer four questions.

## 1.

What games matter today?

---

## 2.

What opportunities stand out?

---

## 3.

Why do they stand out?

---

## 4.

What should I watch?

If a feature doesn't help answer one of those questions, it probably doesn't belong.

---

# The Product Personality

Sports Today should feel like...

a knowledgeable friend.

Not an analyst.

Not a sportsbook.

Not a fantasy expert.

Not television.

The tone should be:

confident

calm

thoughtful

curious

never sensational

never clickbait

never loud

---

# What We Build

Sports Today is built around stories.

Not statistics.

Statistics support stories.

They are not the story.

For example:

Instead of

"Player averages 7.3 rebounds."

Prefer

"This matchup creates one of today's best rebounding opportunities."

Every statistic should answer:

"So what?"

---

# Our Definition of Intelligence

A smart sports application doesn't know the most.

It helps users understand the most.

The difference matters.

Sports Today should surface:

patterns

context

confidence

risk

instead of overwhelming users with raw data.

---

# Trust

Trust is our most valuable feature.

Never exaggerate.

Never overstate confidence.

Never hide uncertainty.

Evidence should always be visible.

Users should understand why recommendations exist.

When information is unavailable, clearly say so.

---

# Explainability

Every recommendation should answer:

Why?

Evidence should always be human-readable.

Confidence should never feel like magic.

The application should never appear to make mysterious decisions.

---

# Confidence

Confidence is earned.

Not declared.

Higher confidence requires:

multiple independent signals

recent evidence

healthy data freshness

good matchup context

Lower confidence should be clearly communicated.

---

# Simplicity

Every screen should answer one primary question.

Avoid trying to answer five questions at once.

If something feels crowded,

remove information,

don't shrink it.

---

# Product Principles

## Surface, don't bury.

Important information should be immediately visible.

---

## Explain, don't impress.

A recommendation without context creates skepticism.

---

## Curate, don't dump.

The application should filter information before presenting it.

---

## Calm, don't shout.

Avoid unnecessary alerts.

Avoid visual noise.

Avoid excessive color.

---

## Guide, don't overwhelm.

The app should feel like it knows where the user should look next.

---

# Long-Term Vision

Sports Today becomes the homepage for sports fans.

Not because it contains everything.

Because it contains the right things.

Users begin their sports day here.

Then continue to games,

highlights,

broadcasts,

or deeper research elsewhere.

# Artificial Intelligence

AI should never exist for its own sake.

It should make the product feel:

smarter

clearer

more personalized

more helpful

Users should notice better recommendations,

not "AI."

---

# Success Metrics

A successful user session is not measured by time spent.

It is measured by clarity gained.

The ideal session is:

Open app.

Understand today's slate.

Choose what to watch.

Leave.

The product succeeds when users feel informed,

not when they stay forever.

---

# Things We Are Not

We are not ESPN.

We are not The Athletic.

We are not StatMuse.

We are not FanDuel.

We are not DraftKings.

We are not Baseball Savant.

We are not Basketball Reference.

We are not a sportsbook.

We are not a fantasy platform.

We are not trying to win with volume.

We win through curation.

---

# Decision Filter

Every new feature should pass these questions.

Does this help users understand today's sports landscape?

Does this reduce cognitive load?

Does this make recommendations more trustworthy?

Does this reinforce our calm, premium experience?

Would someone miss this feature if it disappeared?

If the answer is "no" to most of these,

don't build it.

---

# Product North Star

Sports Today should become the application people instinctively open each morning before the games begin.

It should feel less like checking scores...

...and more like receiving a thoughtful briefing from someone who understands sports exceptionally well.

When people think,

"I wonder what's worth paying attention to today,"

Sports Today should be the first place they go.
